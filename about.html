About

From a young age I was always interested in robotics and computers. My first exposure to this came with tv and movies, movies like robots and cloudy with a chance of meatballs instilled the desire for technical skills at a young age.

In elementary school I joined an after school robotics program, learning to build simple circuits with breadboards, switches, resistors and various other components, eventually leading into arduinos and coding small robots in C#. In middle school, I spent less time working with things of this nature, but in my first two years of high school I completed my high schools digital design and computer coding courses. These included building websites with the absolute basic html and css, as well as programming games in javascript. The skills I learned in these classes carried over very well to college. After high school I did not have a specific goal in mind, so I decided to try community college, taking the 100 level computer science courses. It was through my community college experiences that I rediscovered my love for technical work, as well as my love for mathematics. I completed my Associate of Science through PCC in 2024, and began at Portland State University. Here I dove deeper into the subject of computer science, working towards a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science.
